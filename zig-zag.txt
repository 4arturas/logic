Figure 1 (The Direct Slide)
This is the most common "flow." The Middle Term starts as the Subject and "slides" down to the Predicate.

Plaintext
M ——— P
  \
S ——— M
---------
S ——— P
Figure 2 (The Right Wall)
Here, the Middle Terms are both Predicates. The flow "hits a wall" on the right side.

Plaintext
P ——— M
      |
S ——— M
---------
S ——— P
Figure 3 (The Left Wall)
In this figure, the Middle Terms are both Subjects. The connection is a vertical line on the left.

Plaintext
M ——— P
|
M ——— S
---------
S ——— P
Figure 4 (The Zig-Zag / Reverse)
This is the "Zig-Zag" you likely recall. It moves from the Predicate of the first line to the Subject of the second.

Plaintext
P ——— M
    /
M ——— S
---------
S ——— P
Quick Guide to the Terms
M (Middle Term): The connector that disappears in the conclusion.

S (Subject/Minor Term): The subject of the conclusion.

P (Predicate/Major Term): The predicate of the conclusion.
